Title:
GameGuild: Strategy Sharing and Team-Up Hub

Representative image:
Upload game_guild_poster.png.

Short description:
GameGuild is a Python command-line prototype for a gaming social platform.
Players can create profiles, list their favourite games and roles, publish
strategy guides, search advice by game or keyword, like useful posts, and
create or join team-up requests for future play sessions. New users can ask a
simple help bot how to use the program, and the program also recommends guides,
player matches, and open team requests by comparing favourite games, keywords,
and likes. It is designed for gamers who want a
simple place to share experience and find people to play with, without needing
an online server.

Presentation talking points:
- Problem: Game advice and team finding are often scattered across many apps.
- Solution: A local Python prototype that combines guides, search, likes,
  player profiles, a help bot, team-up requests, and joining a team.
- Advanced concepts: classes, text file I/O, input validation,
  searching, sorting, and recommendation scoring.
- Why it is original: It models a gaming community platform instead of only
  building a single game.
